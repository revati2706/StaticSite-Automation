The Unfilled Heart

In silence deep, it softly beats,

A hollow thrum, where joy retreats.

It longs for light, for warmth once known,

Yet walks a path so much alone.

Not broken — no, but never whole,

A quiet ache within the soul.

It held out hands, received the air,

And found no comfort waiting there.

Still, it hopes in subtle hues,

Of whispered words and morning dews.

One day, perhaps, love may impart,

The piece to fill this unfilled heart.

– Anonymous
